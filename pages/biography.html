Biography


Years of Life


November 15th, 1887-
Georgia Totto O'Keeffe was born in the state of Wisconsin, in a city called "Sun Prairie."

1905-
O'Keefe graduated from high school and began her journey as an artist.

1905 to 1906-
O'Keeffe pursued an art education at the Art Institute of Chicago in Chicago, Illinois.

1907 to 1908-
O'Keeffe expanded her knowledge in painting techniques at the Art Students League in New York.

1908-
O'Keeffe won an award for her oil painting "Dead Rabbit with Copper Pot".

1912-
O'Keeffe attended the University of Virginia for a summer course taught by Alon Bement.

1912 to 1914-
O'Keeffe was an art teacher in Texas schools, in addition to working as Bement's assistant in Virginia during the summer.

1914 to 1915-
O'Keeffe studied in New York at Teacher's College, Colombia University.

Fall 1915-
O'Keeffe taught art at Colombia College in South Carolina.

1916-
O'Keeffe begins contact with Alfred Stieglitz, who admired her drawings from afar.

1924-
O'Keeffe married Stieglitz in New York.

1946-
O'Keeffe's husband and professional partner, Alfred Stieglitz, died in New York.

1949-
O'Keeffe moved to New Mexico, following three years after her husband's death.

1972-
O'Keeffe begins to lose her eyesight.

March 6th, 1986-
O'Keeffe died at the age of 98 in Santa Fe, New Mexico.


Genre of Artwork


Georgia O'Keeffe was a famous American artist who worked with a number of different mediums such as: charcoal, oil paints, watercolor, pencil, and clay. O'Keeffe used the concepts of "realism" and "modernism"(can be seen in her later works) in her artworks.


Group Affiliations


Georgia O'Keeffe met with other artists during her lifetime. Some of these artists included the following: Alfred Stieglitz, John Marin, Marsden Hartley, Ansel Adams (photographer), Diego Rivera, Frida Kahlo (painter), Miguel Covarrubias, and Juan Hamilton.


Typical Subject Matter


Georgia O'Keeffe's typical subject matter in her paintings included a range of things from landscapes, plants, flowers, trees, people, cityscapes, animal bones, and other miscellaneous objects. O'Keeffe drew a lot of inspiration for her paintings during her time living in the beautiful, yet very opposite, states of New York and New Mexico.


More Facts About O'Keeffe


Georgia O'Keeffe lived in a number of places throughout her lifetime. Some of these places include Wisconsin, Chicago, New York, Texas, Virginia, South Carolina, and New Mexico. In addition to her many homes, O'Keeffe loved to travel. Some of the places she traveled to include Japan and Peru.


Even after she began losing her eyesight, she continued to create beautiful art in the form of clay.
